Chapter 2

The First Crack

The morning arrived quietly

not with announcements or expectations

but like a sigh that stretches through empty rooms

Adrian stood by the window

watching the city breathe below him

people moving unaware of how fragile hearts could be

and inside him, a storm waited silently

The fragility of ordinary moments

She had smiled at him yesterday

her hand brushing his as if it were an accident

her words soft and careless

and he had believed in the ordinary

believed that love could always remain

believed that trust was eternal

but betrayal begins in the smallest moments

in a glance too long or too short

in words left unsaid

in secrets that breathe in shadows

He walked the streets alone

trying to find solace in movement

in the rhythm of cars and footsteps

but the world felt hollow

Betrayal arrives disguised as absence

He remembered the first sign

a message on her phone

innocent in appearance

yet carrying a weight he could not name

he had chosen to ignore it

the mind desperate to protect hope

the heart naive enough to trust

But betrayal does not scream

it moves softly

it arrives disguised as carelessness

as tiny shifts that seem meaningless until everything changes

And he realized that betrayal is not only in action

but in silence

in the quiet space where love should have been

Adrian sat by the window

writing in the notebook

tracing the first cracks that would shape everything

that would change him forever
