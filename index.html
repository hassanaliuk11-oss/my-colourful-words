Evening did not arrive loudly it slipped into the room like a secret that had waited too long to be told the sky slowly lost its color blue turning into something tired and unsure as if it too was carrying a memory it could not release
The city outside continued breathing lights blinking without emotion streets moving without meaning people passing with lives that did not touch his and inside the small room on the fourth floor a man sat quietly learning once again how to be alone
Adrian leaned against the wall its cold pressing gently into his back as if reminding him that some things still existed even when warmth did not the room held the smell of old pages and forgotten hours books lay scattered like unfinished thoughts their open mouths unable to speak anymore
Silence lived there now not the peaceful kind but the heavy kind that listens the kind that remembers
Once this room had known another rhythm soft footsteps shared breathing two cups placed too close on a table as if afraid of distance laughter that did not try to impress a voice that said his name like it mattered
Now silence had taken that voice and learned how to imitate it
Adrian closed his eyes and in the darkness behind them memory began to move not loudly not clearly but faithfully
He remembered how love had not ended in fire not in shouting or broken doors not in dramatic last words it had ended the way light leaves a room slowly politely without asking permission
Love had simply grown quieter until one day he realized he was listening to echoes
He opened his eyes again the window reflected a version of him he barely recognized a face shaped by waiting eyes carrying questions they no longer asked aloud time had passed not in years but in weight
Outside the first rain touched the glass soft and careful like it was afraid to disturb something sacred each drop leaving a thin trail as if the sky itself were learning how to cry quietly
He stood and moved closer placing his hand against the cold window imagining for a moment another hand meeting his warm familiar real
The thought hurt not sharply but deeply the way truth often does
He pulled his hand back his breath unsteady and whispered a word meant for no one a word that dissolved before it could become sound
The notebook waited beside him its cover worn by years of holding things he never said it had once been meant for plans for dreams for a future that felt certain now it carried only survival
He opened it slowly pages filled with handwriting that looked older than him sentences crossing each other thoughts beginning bravely and ending in silence
At the top of the page were words written darker than the rest
Love does not always leave sometimes it stays as a shadow
He stared at the line feeling it settle inside his chest a shadow was not darkness it was proof of light proof that something once stood close enough to shape you
That was the cruel beauty of it
He had tried to escape it changing places changing habits changing smiles learning how to answer questions without revealing anything real
but shadows do not belong to places they belong to form they follow you patient faithful unasking
Outside the rain deepened night thickened the city softened into blurred lights and distant motion and inside the room time slowed as if it too were listening
Adrian picked up the pen his hand hesitating before touching the page writing no longer felt like art it felt like breathing through pain
slowly he began again
There are loves that end loudly and there are loves that end by becoming quiet
his hand trembled slightly but he continued
the quiet ones stay longer they move into your silence they rearrange your nights they teach your heart how to ache without making a sound
The words did not heal him but they understood him and that was enough for now
Outside the rain kept falling inside the shadow of love stretched gently across the floor not to hurt him not to leave him but to remind him that once something real had chosen to stay
